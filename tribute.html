J.K. Rowling

It matters not what someone is born, but what they grow to be.

- J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling is a literary genius who has captivated millions with her incredible storytelling and imagination. Her ability to create the magical world of *Harry Potter* from scratch has made her one of the most influential authors of our time.

She has not only enchanted readers but has also inspired me deeply through her resilience, creativity, and philanthropic work.

Some quick facts about Rowlings' life:

  • She moved to Oporto, Portugal in 1991, where she worked as an english teacher until 1993.
  • She married Jorge Arantes, a portuguese journalist, in October 1992. They divorced in December, 1993 and she returned to the UK with her daughter Jessica.
  • J.K. Rowling took inspiration from many iconic places in Oporto, like the São Bento train station and Lello & Irmão Library.
  • She was pitching her crime novel The Cuckoo's Calling to publishers under the pen-name of Robert Galbraith.
  • She received many rejection letters and hilarious responses, and later revealed them on twitter to inspire others. Some of them can be read here.

Why She Stands Out

One thing I admire most about J.K. Rowling is her perseverance. She faced countless rejections before *Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone* was published, yet she never gave up on her dream. Her journey from struggling writer to global icon is a testament to determination and belief in one’s talent.

Another remarkable quality is her ability to inspire hope and courage in readers. Through characters like Harry, Hermione, and Dumbledore, she teaches us about bravery, friendship, and fighting for what’s right. These values have had a profound impact on me.

J.K. Rowling’s generosity is also awe-inspiring. Her philanthropic work, particularly in the fields of children’s welfare and medical research, shows her compassion and commitment to making the world a better place.
